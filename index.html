Big Idea

No perks of being a wallflower

Students should not be afraid to invest time into their personal lives. Building and having strong family and romatic relatinship can benefit the academic success and provide additional support during hard times.

Problem Statement

Students' misperception that active social life always negatively affects academic life

The goal of this study is to prove the opposite

Go to findings

Aim

The aim of this project is to demonstrate how various personal life factors, such as family, friends and romantic relationships, and their interactions influence academic success of a student.

Research gap

Most academic literature focuses on the negative effect of romantic relationships on academic performance. However,a few novel studies have emphasized the positive impact interpersonal factors may have on academic success of the student.

Personal observations

'You know, I'll pass tonight'.
Since I came to NUS, I heard a lot of students saying similar phrases, refusing to go out with friends for the sake of studying, missing out on some wonderful evenings.

The measure academic success of student I chose the variable G3. This variable represents the final average grade for two periods . The grade scale is from 1 to 20 points.

Romantic relationships is a binary variable indicating the presence ("yes") or the absence of romantic relationships ("no").

In this project I used three numerical predictors. Going out with friends measured the frequency on a 5 point Likert scale (1 - very low to 5 - very high). Family relationship quality uses 5-point scale from 1 - very bad to 5 - excellent relationships.
The Data

Which data did I use to test my hypotheses?

The data for this project was taken from the open data source Kaggle.com.
The dataset contains information from 382 secondary school students in Portugal during academic year 2005-2006.
