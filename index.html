Big Idea

No perks of being a wallflower

Students should not be afraid to invest time into their personal lives. Building and having strong family and romatic relatinship can benefit the academic success and provide additional support during hard times.

Aim

The aim of this project is to demonstrate how various personal life factors, such as family, friends and romantic relationships, and their interactions influence academic success of a student.

Research gap

Most academic literature focuses on the negative effect of romantic relationships on academic performance. However, a few novel studies have emphasized the positive impact interpersonal factors may have on academic success of the student.

Problem Statement

Students' misperception that personal life negatively affects academic life. The goal of this study is to prove the opposite.

The measure academic success of student is the variable G3. This variable represents the final average grade for two periods . The grade scale is from 1 to 20 points.

Romantic relationships is a binary variable indicating the presence ("yes") or the absence of romantic relationships ("no").

Going out with friends measured the frequency on a 5 point Likert scale (1 - very low to 5 - very high).

Family relationship quality uses 5-point scale from 1 - very bad to 5 - excellent relationships.
Meta data information

The provenance of the data

The data for this project was taken from the open data source Kaggle.com.
The dataset contains information from 382 secondary school students in Portugal during academic year 2005-2006.

Results

My findings


On this graph, you can see that the better the relationship between the student and the family is, the higher are the student's marks. Therefore, good relations with your siblings and parents have a significant positive effect on your GPAs (p < 0.01).



The plot above shows the interaction effect of family relatinship and the presence of romatic relationship (e.g. dating) on the students' GPA. The main take-away from this visualization is that in case of poor relationship with the family, the romantic relationship can compensate the lacking support: the average grade of dating students is 2 points higher than of the student without romantic relationship, holding the poor family relatinship for constant.



The third linechart below visualizes the effect of going out frequency on the students' average marks. The variables have a significant negative linear relationship, meaning that the more often one goes out with friends, the lower are their marks (p < 0.01).
